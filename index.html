& so the story begins

All my adult life has been in search of looking for answers to questions concerning nature of computers with respect to people.

Questions like how can I deeply understand what is it about the medium of computing that can extend people’s capabilities. What is it about the orchestration of bits and bytes on screen that can give people the ability to do what they want.

I’ve spent the past 3 years on a self-directed learning journey trying to understand that by reading philosophy of tech papers, design theories and any thing that connects human behaviour with technology all by myself with just my curiosity to drive me.

Through this I have honed my research skills and have learnt to storm bouts of fear, anxiety and how to continue to work with uncertainty.

Though I have gained a lot of perspective via my readings on the relationship between software and people and the kind of work I’d like to do I realize the urgency to materialize my ideas, perspectives by communicating with people and learning concrete skills to make them tangible.

My research has culminated into the beginnings of a framework that I'd like to develop and apply at the bootcamp. It is based on theory called social practice theory which claims people's behaviour is a result of routinized type of behaviors, typically & habitually performed and shared and created within a group of people in a society.

With my framework people would be able to answer what are some routinized ways of being (thinking, feeling, doing, saying) that we uniquely do with the computing medium. A way or rather a language for people to talk about how their ui widgets, patterns and task flows cause routinized ways of doing, saying, feeling, thinking things with long term implications

When I came across your course I felt it was exactly what I needed to do that, especially because of the community aspect.

Coding gives you the freedom to make anything you want anytime anywhere, that is an unprecedented power to have.

With this power I’ll have this lifelong skill to work on any problem and materialize outcomes that I desire to sustain a living.

I can see myself doing that for the rest of my life, a life of constant learning and experimentation, invention and I would like to kickstart doing that by learning that skill y’all and kickstarting my career.
